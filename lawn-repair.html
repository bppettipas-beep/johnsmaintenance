Lawn damage can occur for many reasons, including pet spots, heavy traffic, disease, drought stress, or winter damage. Whatever the cause, our experienced team has the knowledge and tools to diagnose the problem and implement the most effective repair solution for your specific situation.

Common Lawn Problems We Fix

Dead Spots & Patches

  • Pet urine damage
  • Chemical burns
  • Disease-affected areas
  • Drought stress damage

Thin or Bare Areas

  • Heavy traffic wear
  • Shade-damaged grass
  • Compacted soil areas
  • Poor growing conditions

Winter Damage

  • Salt damage from roadways
  • Ice and snow damage
  • Freeze-thaw cycles
  • Snow mold problems

Drainage Issues

  • Standing water problems
  • Soggy, muddy areas
  • Poor soil conditions
  • Grading problems

Our Lawn Repair Process

1

Problem Assessment

We thoroughly evaluate the damaged areas to identify the root cause and determine the best repair approach for your specific situation.

2

Soil Preparation

Proper soil preparation is key to successful repairs. We address compaction, drainage, and soil quality issues before any repair work begins.

3

Repair Implementation

Using the best method for each situation, whether seeding, sodding, or a combination approach, we execute repairs with care and expertise.

4

Establishment Care

We provide guidance and follow-up care recommendations to ensure your repaired areas establish successfully and blend seamlessly with the rest of your lawn.

Repair Methods We Use

The Right Method for Every Situation

We select the most appropriate repair method based on the size of the damaged area, timing, and your budget.

Spot Seeding

Cost-effective for smaller damaged areas. Ideal when the damage is localized and surrounding turf is healthy.

Patch Sodding

Instant results for high-visibility areas. Perfect when you need the lawn looking great quickly.

Overseeding

Thickening thin areas throughout the entire lawn. Great for lawns that have become sparse over time.

Combination Approach

Using multiple methods for comprehensive repair. Best for lawns with varied damage types and severities.
